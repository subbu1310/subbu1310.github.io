About Me

I’m a Process Design Engineer with FEED experience working on energy and industrial process projects. I enjoy the early design phase of projects — where process fundamentals, safety considerations, and real-world constraints come together to support practical engineering decisions.

In my role, I’ve worked on developing and validating process simulation models, preparing mass and energy balances, generating stream data tables, and supporting equipment-related design activities. As part of FEED deliverables, I’ve reviewed key process documentation such as PFDs and P&IDs, ensuring alignment between process intent, design requirements, and documentation standards. I’ve also contributed to process safety work, including PSV sizing activities and heat transfer calculations related to nitrogen blanketing to ensure positive pressure during safe shutdown scenarios.

I enjoy working with data and use tools like Python to automate calculations, analyze large datasets, and present engineering insights more clearly. I’m continuously building my technical depth through focused learning in areas like pump and valve sizing, compressors, heat exchangers, and P&ID drafting standards.

Projects / Proof of Work

Fredericia Pyrolysis Plant — FEED Process Simulation & Design Deliverables

FEED study supporting early-stage design decisions and engineering documentation (Fredericia, Denmark).

  • Built the initial steady-state process simulation model in DWSIM, forming the basis for early design evaluation.
  • Generated stream data tables and key process outputs from the model to support design reviews.
  • Developed early deliverables including BFD (MS Visio), then progressed to PFD as the design matured.
  • Supported FEED documentation by reviewing P&IDs and coordinating review comments with stakeholders.
  • Collaborated closely with system owners, performing an initial technical review before final verification.

Deliverables: DWSIM model basis · Stream data tables · BFD · PFD · P&ID review comments

Randers Pyrolysis Plant — PSV Revalidation & Blanketing Support

FEED/design support focused on relief system validation and nitrogen blanketing calculations.

  • Participated in PSV review and revalidation for an existing relief design from earlier phases.
  • Rechecked sizing basis against updated operating scenarios, flow rates, and assumptions.
  • Reviewed PSV methodology and documentation with reference to API 520 principles.
  • Supported senior engineers with nitrogen blanketing calculations for inert conditions during shutdown.

Deliverables: PSV review notes · Blanketing calculation support

Randers Pyrolysis Plant — Root Cause Analysis Using SCADA Data

Operational troubleshooting using historical SCADA/production data to identify trends linked to performance issues.

  • Worked with large operational datasets (3 months of time-series values; millions of rows) from SCADA exports.
  • Extracted and structured key events from sensor signals to identify stoppages, frequency, and duration.
  • Used Python automation (cleaning + plotting) to speed up analysis vs manual Excel workflows.
  • Created trend plots (e.g., oil production vs time) and repeatable event metrics.
  • Delivered a reusable workflow so engineers could input new datasets and reproduce graphs and metrics.

Deliverables: Automated analysis workflow · Trend graphs · Event/stoppage metrics
