About Me

I’m a Process Design Engineer with FEED experience working on energy and industrial process projects. I’m especially interested in how engineering fundamentals, process safety, and real operating constraints come together in practical systems.

In my role, I’ve worked on developing and validating process simulation models, preparing mass and energy balances, generating stream data tables, and supporting equipment-related design activities. As part of FEED deliverables, I’ve developed and reviewed key process documentation such as PFDs and P&IDs, ensuring alignment between process intent, design requirements, and documentation standards. I’ve also contributed to process safety work, including PSV sizing activities and heat transfer calculations related to nitrogen blanketing to ensure positive pressure during safe shutdown scenarios.

I also enjoy working with data and use tools like Python to automate calculations, analyze large datasets, and present engineering insights more clearly. I’m continuously building my technical depth through focused learning in areas like pump and valve sizing, compressors, heat exchangers, and P&ID drafting standards.

From Review to Ownership

A short summary of how my responsibilities and technical depth have progressed.

During my professional experience, I was primarily involved in simulations, calculations, reviewing process documents such as P&IDs and PSV sizing sheets, and supporting senior engineers with pressure-drop calculations. This helped me understand how design calculations and engineering decisions are reviewed, checked, and validated in an industrial environment.

Over time, I realized that while I could support, interpret, and review design deliverables, I wanted to strengthen my ability to create them independently. After my role, I deliberately focused on learning core process engineering deliverables in depth. Some of the key areas I focused on are listed below.

  • PSV: Earlier, I reviewed PSV sizing Excel sheets. Now, I can independently prepare Excel sheets for PSV sizing and clearly understand the basis and assumptions.
  • P&ID: Earlier, I was mainly a reviewer and interpreter of P&IDs. Now, I can draw and develop P&IDs, not just interpret them.
  • Pressure drop calculations: Earlier, I supported senior engineers in building models for pressure-drop calculations. Now, I can perform pressure-drop calculations independently with stronger fundamentals in flow through pipes and valves.
  • Heat transfer: Earlier, my understanding was limited to heat duty calculations. Now, my understanding of heat transfer coefficients has increased significantly, helping me connect calculations with physical behavior and design decisions.
  • Equipment sizing: Earlier, my exposure to equipment sizing was limited. Now, I can confidently size pumps, valves, and heat exchangers using Excel sheets as well as simulation software.
  • Working style: I apply lean principles to work in a structured manner with clear assumptions, clean documentation, and reusable templates.
